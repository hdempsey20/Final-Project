Creating Walt Disney World

Walt Disney and his brother created Disney Land in California first before creating Disney World in Florida. However, Walt Disney had these great and big ideas that did not fit in the space they had at Disney Land, so he decided to create Disney World. Walt Disney started buying separate pieces of land under different and fake company names. He thought if he bought one big piece of land people would get suspicious. At first, the focus was mainly on the Magic Kingdom with its new attractions and facilities in the park. The second park was Epcot, followed by MGM Studios which is now called Hollywood Studios.

Walt Disney World Today

Today, Walt Disney World in total has had over 700 million visitors from all over the world. There are seven Disney resorts around the world: Disney Land, Disney World, Disney's Aulani Resort in Hawaii, Disneyland Paris, Hong Kong Disneyland, Shanghai Disney Resort, and Tokyo Disney Resort. Even though Walt Disney passed away before Disney World was finished, his ideas were brought to life by the people behind creating Disney with him.

About Walt Disney

Walt Disney was born on December 5, 1901 in Hermosa, Illinois. Walt had four siblings: 3 brothers and 1 sister. After he was born, the Disney family moved to Marceline, Missouri where he lived for most of his childhood. From an early age, Walt Disney had an interest in art; he pursued this by studying art at his high school. When he was 16 years old, Walt joined the Red Cross and was sent over to France to drive an ambulance. When he returned, Walt and his brother founded Walt Disney Productions.
