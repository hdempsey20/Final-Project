Magic Kingdom

Magic Kingdom

The Magic Kingdom is the central park at Walt Disney World. It was the first park in the Florida project, opening on October 1st, 1971 after the death of Walt Disney. It has six different lands: Main Street USA, Adventureland, Liberty Square, Frontierland, Fantasyland, and Tomorrowland.

Epcot

Epcot

Epcot stands for Experimental Prototype Community of Tomorrow. It contains a Future World and World Showcase. There are 11 countries involved in World Showcase: USA, Canada, Mexico, Norway, England, Japan, China, Morocco, Germany, France, and Italy. There is also an Africa Outpost to stop and buy merchandise at.

Hollywood Studios

Hollywood Studios

Hollywood Studios was orginally named MGM Studios when it first opened in May of 1989. It's theme is entertainment and show buisness. One of it's most popular attractions, Rock 'n' Roller Coaster, goes from 0-57 mph in 2 seconds. Another popular ride, Tower of Terror, has a 13-story drop.

Animal Kingdom

Animal Kingdom

Animal Kingdom is the largest of the four parks. However, most of the space is for the animals, so the public can not see most of the park. It is home to many animals, some of which are part of endangered species. Animal Kingdom has different continents and lands such as Asia, Africa, and DinoLand U.S.A.
